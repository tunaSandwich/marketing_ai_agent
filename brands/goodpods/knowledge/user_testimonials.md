## Common Use Cases

### The Daily Commuter
- Needs 20-45 minute episodes
- Prefers educational or news content
- Values consistent quality and regular updates
- Uses queue management for seamless listening

### The Weekend Binger
- Enjoys long-form content and series
- Discovers through curated collections
- Shares favorite episodes with friends
- Explores different genres based on mood

### The Social Listener
- Follows friends and tastemakers
- Participates in episode discussions
- Shares discoveries with their network
- Values community recommendations over algorithms

### The Organization Enthusiast
- Manages large subscription lists
- Uses tags and folders for categorization
- Imports from multiple previous apps
- Appreciates clean, ad-free interface

### The Genre Explorer
- Uses collections to discover new categories
- Follows expert curators in specific genres
- Appreciates diverse recommendation sources
- Values quality over quantity in suggestions

## Community Building
- Users frequently mention the non-toxic, thoughtful community
- Episode discussions tend to be substantive and respectful
- Friend recommendations feel more personal and relevant
- Curators are seen as trusted taste-makers rather than algorithms

## Key Value Propositions
1. **Trust-based discovery**: Friends and curators vs. algorithms
2. **Organization tools**: Better management than platform players
3. **Community aspect**: Discussions and social sharing
4. **Quality focus**: Curated over quantity
5. **Clean experience**: No ads, thoughtful design
