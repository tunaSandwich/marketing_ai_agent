# Course Plan: "The Psychology of Happiness"

## Course Purpose
A practical and evidence-based course on what truly makes people happy. You'll explore insights from psychologists, neuroscientists, and philosophers on fulfillment, meaning, and emotional well-being. Through stories, science, and strategies, you'll learn how happiness can be cultivated — not just pursued — across work, relationships, and everyday life.

## Course Strategy

### Foundational Concepts (Beginner Level)
- What is happiness? Hedonic (pleasure) vs eudaimonic (meaning) well-being
- The science of positive psychology: Martin Seligman's PERMA model
- Social relationships as the #1 predictor of happiness (Harvard Study of Adult Development)
- Common myths about happiness (money, achievement, circumstances vs. reality)
- Set point theory and adaptation
- Basic evidence-based practices: gratitude, mindfulness, savoring, acts of kindness
- Making happiness science accessible through engaging storytelling

### Core Mechanisms/Systems (Intermediate Level)
- Flow states and engagement (Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi)
- Meaning and purpose: Viktor Frankl's logotherapy and existential psychology
- Character strengths and virtues (VIA classification)
- The neuroscience of happiness: dopamine, serotonin, reward systems, neuroplasticity
- Happiness across cultures: universal vs. culturally-specific elements
- Work satisfaction and calling
- The role of community and belonging
- Self-compassion and acceptance (Kristin Neff)
- Resilience and post-traumatic growth

### Research Frontiers/Debates (Advanced Level)
- Philosophy of well-being: objective vs. subjective theories, hedonism vs. eudaimonism
- Happiness economics: Can governments measure and promote happiness?
- The hedonic treadmill debate: Can we overcome adaptation?
- Genetic set points: How much is happiness predetermined?
- Technology, social media, and modern challenges to well-being
- The paradox of happiness pursuit: When trying too hard backfires
- Existential and philosophical perspectives on the good life
- Suffering, adversity, and the role of negative emotions
- Death awareness and mortality salience
- Collective well-being vs. individual happiness

## Quality Assurance for Psychology of Happiness Course

### MUST HAVE:
✅ Clear psychological/neuroscience credentials (Professor of Psychology, PhD, Research Center Director)
✅ Interview format (expert being interviewed, not solo host monologue)
✅ Academic affiliation or peer-reviewed research mentioned
✅ Evidence-based approaches (not just opinion or anecdote)
✅ Clear expertise in happiness, positive psychology, or related field

### RED FLAGS:
❌ No credentials mentioned in description
❌ Life coaches or motivational speakers without research background
❌ Self-help content without scientific grounding
❌ "Spiritual" or "manifesting" content without psychological science
❌ Amateur podcasts with hosts discussing happiness casually
❌ Solo host explaining happiness from self-help books
❌ No identifiable expert guest
❌ Generic wellness advice without research backing

### Format Indicators:
- ✅ GOOD: "[Name], Professor of Psychology at [University]"
- ✅ GOOD: "Director of [Happiness/Well-being Center]"
- ✅ GOOD: "Author of [research-based book], published studies on..."
- ✅ GOOD: "[Host] interviews [Psychologist]"
- ❌ BAD: "We discuss happiness" (no guest mentioned)
- ❌ BAD: "I share my happiness tips" (solo host, no expertise)
- ❌ BAD: "Life coach shares secrets to happiness" (no research background)

## Expected Episode Progression

### Beginner (6 episodes):
1. What is happiness? The science of positive psychology (Martin Seligman or similar)
2. Relationships: The Harvard Study shows social connections are key (Robert Waldinger)
3. The neuroscience of happiness: How our brains process reward and well-being
4. Gratitude and mindfulness: Evidence-based practices that work
5. Common myths about happiness: Money, achievement, and what really matters
6. Flow: Finding engagement and joy in activities (Csikszentmihalyi or similar)

**Format:** Well-known hosts (Huberman, Lex Fridman, Tim Ferriss) interviewing leading psychologists
**Guests:** Laurie Santos, Robert Waldinger, Martin Seligman, Andrew Huberman explaining research
**Tone:** "Help me understand what science says about happiness and how I can apply it"

### Intermediate (6 episodes):
1. Meaning and purpose: Viktor Frankl's logotherapy and finding your "why"
2. Character strengths: Discovering and using your unique strengths
3. Self-compassion and acceptance: Kristin Neff's research
4. Happiness across cultures: What's universal and what's culturally specific
5. Resilience and post-traumatic growth: Growing through adversity
6. Work and calling: Finding fulfillment in what you do

**Format:** Psychology podcasts or academic discussions with specialists
**Guests:** Researchers discussing their specific areas of expertise
**Tone:** "Teach me the deeper mechanisms and how to cultivate lasting well-being"

### Advanced (6 episodes):
1. Philosophy of well-being: What is the good life? Objective vs subjective theories
2. Happiness economics: Can governments measure and promote happiness?
3. The hedonic treadmill: Can we escape adaptation? The set-point debate
4. Technology and modern happiness: Social media, loneliness, digital well-being
5. The paradox of happiness pursuit: When trying too hard backfires
6. Existential perspectives: Suffering, mortality, and authentic happiness

**Format:** Technical discussions with philosophers, economists, or cutting-edge researchers
**Guests:** Philosophers, behavioral economists, existential psychologists
**Tone:** "Show me the complex debates and frontier questions in well-being science"
