# Women's Health and Hormones - Course Plan

## Course Vision
A science-based journey through women's health across the lifespan, taught by leading doctors, researchers, and medical experts. From understanding the menstrual cycle to navigating menopause, this course demystifies hormones and empowers listeners with evidence-based knowledge.

## Level Progression Strategy

### BEGINNER: Understanding the Basics
**Goal:** Build foundational knowledge of female hormones and reproductive biology
**Topics to cover:**
- How the menstrual cycle works (hormones, phases, what's normal)
- Overview of key hormones: estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, cortisol
- Puberty to menopause: the lifecycle overview
- How hormones affect mood, energy, and metabolism
- Basic reproductive anatomy and physiology
- Common myths vs. facts

**Ideal format:** Well-known science communicators (Huberman, Attia) interviewing OB/GYNs or endocrinologists

### INTERMEDIATE: Mechanisms and Applications
**Goal:** Deep dive into specific systems, interventions, and conditions
**Topics to cover:**
- Birth control: mechanisms, types, hormonal effects
- Fertility science: ovulation, conception, factors affecting fertility
- Pregnancy hormones and physiological changes
- Common conditions: PCOS, endometriosis, fibroids
- Hormones and metabolism, weight, insulin sensitivity
- Exercise and nutrition across the menstrual cycle
- Hormone testing and interpretation

**Ideal format:** Medical podcasts with specialist doctors (reproductive endocrinologists, OB/GYNs with research backgrounds)

### ADVANCED: Research Frontiers and Complex Topics
**Goal:** Cutting-edge research, controversial debates, nuanced medical discussions
**Topics to cover:**
- Perimenopause and menopause: latest research and management
- Hormone replacement therapy (HRT): risks, benefits, personalization
- Hormones and brain health, cognitive function, Alzheimer's risk
- Microbiome and hormone interactions
- Egg freezing, IVF, and assisted reproduction technologies
- Environmental endocrine disruptors
- Personalized medicine approaches to women's health
- Ongoing research and unsolved questions

**Ideal format:** Academic researchers, leading specialists, authors of medical studies

## Expertise Indicators to Look For

### Medical Credentials:
- MD (Medical Doctor), DO (Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine)
- OB/GYN (Board-certified obstetrician-gynecologist)
- Reproductive Endocrinologist
- Endocrinologist specializing in women's health
- MPH (Master of Public Health) with women's health focus

### Academic Credentials:
- PhD in reproductive biology, endocrinology, physiology
- Professor at medical school or university
- Research scientist at academic medical center
- Department chair/director at university hospital

### Professional Authority:
- Director of fertility clinic or women's health center
- Founder of women's health organization/practice
- Chief Medical Officer at women's health company
- Author of medical textbooks or research papers on women's health
- Regular contributor to medical journals (NEJM, JAMA, etc.)

### Book Authors:
- Authors of evidence-based books on women's health
- Medical doctors who've written for general audiences
- Researchers who've published accessible science books

### Red Flags to Avoid:
- "Wellness coaches" without medical credentials
- Alternative medicine practitioners making unfounded claims
- Spiritual/energy-based interpretations of hormones
- Anti-science rhetoric or vaccine skepticism
- Supplement salespeople posing as experts
- "Natural hormone balancing" without medical backing

## Expected Episode Characteristics

### Beginner Episodes Should Feature:
- Accessible explanations from credentialed doctors
- Broad overview topics (menstrual cycle 101, hormones explained)
- Well-known podcast hosts interviewing respected MDs
- Clear, jargon-free explanations with medical accuracy
- Emphasis on "what every woman should know"

### Intermediate Episodes Should Feature:
- Specialists discussing specific conditions or interventions
- More technical depth (mechanisms, treatment options)
- OB/GYNs or reproductive endocrinologists with clinical experience
- Evidence-based discussions of pros/cons
- Practical applications of scientific knowledge

### Advanced Episodes Should Feature:
- Leading researchers discussing their studies
- Cutting-edge topics (latest menopause research, HRT debates)
- Professors, department heads, or research directors
- Nuanced discussions of complex medical topics
- Future directions in women's health research

## Quality Assurance Focus

For this course, we must ensure:
1. ALL episodes feature credentialed medical professionals (MD, DO, PhD in relevant field)
2. No "wellness" pseudoscience or unproven claims
3. Evidence-based medicine only
4. Clear expertise signals (university, hospital, research institution)
5. Interview format with identifiable expert guests
6. Diversity of podcasts (18 different shows)
7. Progressive learning journey from basics to advanced research
8. Trustworthy sources that women can rely on for their health decisions

The stakes are high for medical topics - we must be especially rigorous about credentials and scientific accuracy.
