# Course Plan: Stock Market Investing - Timeless Principles

## Course Overview

**Title:** Stock Market Investing: Timeless Principles
**Slug:** stock-market-investing
**Target Audience:** Aspiring investors, beginners to intermediate investors, anyone wanting to build long-term wealth
**Core Goal:** Learn timeless investing philosophies and strategies from legendary investors and modern practitioners. Focus on enduring principles, not market timing or speculation.

## Learning Progression

### Beginner Level: Stock Market Fundamentals
- **What:** Introduction to the stock market, how it works, basic concepts
- **Focus:** Stocks vs. bonds, P/E ratios, dividends, index funds, investing vs. speculation
- **Guest Types:** Legendary investors on accessible podcasts (Tim Ferriss, Lex Fridman, Masters in Business)
- **Listener Outcome:** Understand what the stock market is, how it functions, and why long-term investing works

### Intermediate Level: Valuation, Risk & Behavior
- **What:** Deep dives into valuation methods, risk management, portfolio construction, investor psychology
- **Focus:** Value vs. growth investing, behavioral finance, market cycles, fundamental analysis
- **Guest Types:** Professional investors, fund managers, authors (Invest Like the Best, Capital Allocators, TIP)
- **Listener Outcome:** Learn how to analyze stocks, manage risk, and understand the psychology of investing

### Advanced Level: Wealth-Building Strategies & Philosophy
- **What:** Long-term wealth compounding, advanced portfolio design, mental models from legends
- **Focus:** Asset allocation at scale, economic cycles, macro investing, alternative strategies
- **Guest Types:** Billionaire investors, hedge fund managers, economic thinkers
- **Listener Outcome:** Think like an investor with decades of experience, build a personal investing philosophy

✅ "Managed $X billion" or "$X billion AUM"
✅ "XX-year track record"
✅ "Returned X% annually over Y years"
✅ "Outperformed S&P 500 by X%"
✅ Author of investing classics (The Intelligent Investor, etc.)

**Professional Authority:**
✅ "CIO" (Chief Investment Officer)
✅ "Partner at [Top VC/PE Firm]"
✅ "Former fund manager"
✅ "Professor of Finance" at Wharton, Harvard Business School, Chicago Booth, etc.
✅ "Financial analyst with X years experience"
✅ Created influential investment frameworks or strategies

**Track Record Indicators:**
✅ "Built [Fund] from $X to $Y"
✅ "Legendary investor"
✅ "Beat the market for X years"
✅ "Invested early in [successful companies]"
✅ "Managed X fund for Y decades"

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
❌ "Financial advisor" without specific fund or track record
❌ Day trading focus or get-rich-quick schemes
❌ Crypto-only focus without traditional investing credentials
❌ Motivational speakers without actual investing experience
❌ Generic "wealth coach" without portfolio management experience
❌ Host monologue format (not interviewing an expert)
❌ "Stock picker" or "market timer" without proven long-term track record
❌ Speculation/gambling framing rather than long-term investing
❌ Technical analysis without fundamental backing
❌ Promises of "guaranteed returns" or "beat the market easily"

## Episode Evaluation Criteria

### Quality Scoring Framework

**⭐⭐⭐ EXCELLENT (Priority Selection):**
- Legendary investor (Buffett, Dalio, Marks, Munger, Bogle) being interviewed
- Clear track record: managed billions OR decades of proven returns
- From Top 200 podcast OR respected investing-specific podcast
- Clear interview format (not host monologue)
- Focus on timeless principles, investment philosophy, mental models
- Episode description mentions specific credentials or investment achievements

**⭐⭐ GOOD (Solid Backup):**
- Professional fund manager or institutional investor with clear credentials
- Finance professor at top business school with investing expertise
- Author of respected investing book with practical experience
- Specific investment philosophy or strategy explained with real-world examples
- Clear track record even if less famous

**⭐ ACCEPTABLE (Use Only if Needed):**
- Some investment experience mentioned but less established
- Less known podcast but legitimate investing focus
- Relevant insights but not ideal credentials

**❌ REJECT (Never Use):**
- No clear investing credentials or track record
- Day trading or short-term speculation focus
- Get-rich-quick schemes or promises of easy returns
- Host monologue or casual discussion without expert
- Generic business advice without investment depth
- Technical analysis only (no fundamental backing)
- Crypto-focused without traditional investing foundation
- "Expert" or "guru" without verifiable fund management or portfolio
- Motivational/spiritual framing without substance

### Investing-Specific Quality Checks

For each episode, verify:
- [ ] Guest has actually managed money (fund, portfolio, or personal wealth with documented results)
- [ ] Episode description mentions specific investment philosophy or strategy
- [ ] Clear interview format (not host explaining concepts from books)
- [ ] Investor/fund manager role explicitly stated
- [ ] Some indication of track record (returns, fund size, years managing money)
- [ ] Focus on long-term investing, not trading or speculation
- [ ] Timeless principles emphasized over market timing or hot stocks

## Final Notes

- Investing is about long-term wealth creation - prioritize patience over excitement
- Look for specific track records (returns, AUM, years managing money)
- Diversity of investment styles: value, growth, index, macro, behavioral
- Diversity of firm types: hedge funds, mutual funds, index funds, institutional
- Interview format is critical - we want investor voices, not hosts explaining
- If description doesn't mention specific fund managed or clear role, be skeptical
- Legendary voices (even older interviews) are timeless because principles endure
- Avoid anyone promising easy money or guaranteed returns
- Focus on mental models, behavioral insights, and fundamental analysis
