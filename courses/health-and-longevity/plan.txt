# Course Plan: "Health and Longevity"

## Course Purpose
A science-focused course on the principles and discoveries behind a long, healthy life. You'll learn about nutrition, exercise, sleep, stress regulation, and cutting-edge research in aging biology and preventive medicine. With insights from top scientists and physicians, this course connects daily habits with the molecular science of vitality — so you can understand (and extend) the span of your health.

## Course Strategy

### Foundational Concepts (Beginner Level)
- What is longevity science? The basics of healthspan vs lifespan
- The "big four" pillars: nutrition, exercise, sleep, stress
- How aging works at the cellular level (accessible introduction)
- Common health myths vs. evidence-based practices
- Preventive medicine basics: Why early intervention matters
- Making complex biology accessible through expert storytelling

### Core Systems/Mechanisms (Intermediate Level)
- Exercise science deep-dive: strength training, cardiovascular health, zone training
- Nutritional biochemistry: macronutrients, micronutrients, metabolic health
- Sleep architecture and optimization: circadian rhythms, sleep stages, recovery
- Stress physiology: cortisol, HRV, nervous system regulation
- Cardiovascular health: blood pressure, cholesterol, heart disease prevention
- Brain health: neuroplasticity, cognitive decline prevention, mental performance
- Metabolic health: insulin sensitivity, glucose regulation, Type 2 diabetes prevention
- Body composition: muscle mass, fat loss, body recomposition

### Cutting-Edge Research/Frontiers (Advanced Level)
- Molecular biology of aging: cellular senescence, telomeres, DNA damage
- NAD+ and sirtuins: energy metabolism and longevity pathways
- Autophagy and cellular cleanup: fasting, time-restricted eating
- Mitochondrial health and energy production
- Epigenetics: How lifestyle changes gene expression and biological age
- Senolytics and senescent cells: clearing "zombie cells"
- Caloric restriction and longevity: what the research shows
- Rapamycin, metformin, and pharmaceutical interventions
- Biomarker tracking and personalized medicine
- Future of longevity research: what's on the horizon

## Quality Assurance for Health & Longevity Course

### MUST HAVE:
✅ MD or PhD credentials clearly stated
✅ Academic affiliation (university, research institute, hospital)
✅ Published research or clinical practice mentioned
✅ Interview format (expert being interviewed, not solo host)
✅ Evidence-based approach (mentions studies, research, data)
✅ Specific expertise in the topic being discussed

### RED FLAGS:
❌ No credentials mentioned in description
❌ "Wellness influencer" or "biohacker" without scientific background
❌ Supplement salesperson or company owner as primary identity
❌ Alternative medicine without scientific grounding
❌ "Natural" or "holistic" without MD/PhD backing
❌ Solo host monologue about health topics
❌ No identifiable expert guest
❌ Pseudoscience or unproven claims
❌ Anti-vaccine or conspiracy theory content

### Format Indicators:
- ✅ GOOD: "[Name], MD/PhD, Professor at [University]"
- ✅ GOOD: "Research scientist at [Institution]"
- ✅ GOOD: "[Host] interviews [Expert]"
- ✅ GOOD: "Board certified [specialty]"
- ✅ GOOD: "Published research on [topic]"
- ❌ BAD: "We discuss health" (no guest mentioned)
- ❌ BAD: "I explain how to..." (solo host)
- ❌ BAD: "Health coach shares tips" (no credentials)
- ❌ BAD: Just "expert" or "specialist" without specifics

## Expected Episode Progression

### Beginner (6 episodes):
1. Introduction to longevity science: What determines healthspan?
2. The four pillars: nutrition, exercise, sleep, stress (overview)
3. How aging works: Cellular and molecular basics made accessible
4. Exercise fundamentals: What types of movement matter most?
5. Nutrition basics: What does the science actually say?
6. Sleep essentials: Why sleep is foundational to health

**Format:** Well-known hosts (Huberman, Rogan, Fridman, Ferriss) interviewing top longevity experts
**Guests:** Peter Attia, Rhonda Patrick, David Sinclair, Matthew Walker, etc.
**Tone:** "Make complex biology accessible and show me why this matters for my life"

### Intermediate (6 episodes):
1. Exercise science deep-dive: Strength, cardio, zone training, VO2 max
2. Nutritional biochemistry: Macros, micros, metabolic health
3. Sleep architecture: Circadian rhythms, sleep stages, optimization
4. Stress and the nervous system: HRV, cortisol, resilience
5. Cardiovascular health: Prevention strategies backed by science
6. Brain health and neuroplasticity: Cognitive longevity

**Format:** Health-focused podcasts or academic discussions
**Guests:** Exercise scientists, nutrition PhDs, cardiologists, neuroscientists
**Tone:** "Teach me the mechanisms and how to apply them"

### Advanced (6 episodes):
1. Molecular biology of aging: Cellular senescence, DNA damage, repair
2. NAD+, sirtuins, and longevity pathways: The cutting edge
3. Autophagy and fasting: Time-restricted eating, caloric restriction
4. Epigenetics and biological age: How lifestyle changes gene expression
5. Mitochondrial health and energy: Optimizing cellular powerhouses
6. Senolytics, rapamycin, and longevity interventions: What's next?

**Format:** Research-focused discussions with scientists at the frontier
**Guests:** Aging biology researchers, longevity scientists, clinical researchers
**Tone:** "Show me the current research and where the field is headed"
