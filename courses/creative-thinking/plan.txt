# CREATIVE THINKING COURSE - COMPREHENSIVE STRATEGY & SEARCH PLAN

## COURSE OVERVIEW
**Title:** Creative Thinking — Unlocking Innovation with Practical Creativity Frameworks
**Slug:** creative-thinking
**Target:** People who want to understand and develop creative thinking skills through expert-backed frameworks

---

## COURSE PROGRESSION STRATEGY

### BEGINNER LEVEL - Foundations of Creative Thinking
**Goals:** Demystify creativity, dispel myths, introduce foundational concepts

**Core Questions:**
- What is creativity and can it be learned?
- How do creative people actually think?
- What are the myths vs realities of creative genius?
- What are basic frameworks for generating ideas?
- How do constraints enable creativity?

**Ideal Guests:**
- Psychologists who study creativity
- Well-known creative practitioners (designers, artists, inventors)
- Authors of creativity books explaining their frameworks
- Innovation professors at top universities
- Design thinking pioneers (IDEO, d.school founders)

**Episode Types:**
- Accessible explanations from Lex Fridman, Tim Ferriss, Rick Rubin interviewing creative masters
- TED talks or TED Radio Hour featuring creativity researchers
- How I Built This founders explaining their creative process
- Psychologists on Hidden Brain explaining creativity science

---

### INTERMEDIATE LEVEL - Techniques & Applications
**Goals:** Learn specific frameworks, understand divergent/convergent thinking, see real-world applications

**Core Questions:**
- How does design thinking actually work?
- What are proven techniques for divergent and convergent thinking?
- How do organizations foster creativity?
- How do you apply creative frameworks to business problems?
- What role does failure play in creative innovation?

**Ideal Guests:**
- Design thinking practitioners (IDEO, Stanford d.school)
- Innovation consultants with track records
- Founders who built innovative companies
- Product leaders from innovative companies
- Organizational psychologists studying creativity in teams

**Episode Types:**
- Masters of Scale featuring innovation leaders
- Lenny's Podcast with product innovators
- a16z discussing innovation frameworks
- Domain experts on specialized podcasts explaining specific techniques

---

### ADVANCED LEVEL - Research Frontiers & Complex Applications
**Goals:** Understand the neuroscience, explore research debates, examine creativity in complex systems

**Core Questions:**
- What does neuroscience reveal about creative insight?
- How do breakthrough innovations actually happen?
- What are the cognitive mechanisms of creativity?
- How does creativity work in AI/technology/science?
- What are the limits and future of creativity research?

**Ideal Guests:**
- Neuroscientists studying creative cognition
- Creativity researchers publishing in top journals
- Nobel Prize winners or breakthrough innovators
- AI researchers exploring computational creativity
- Cognitive scientists studying insight and problem-solving

**Episode Types:**
- Huberman Lab on creativity neuroscience
- Sean Carroll's Mindscape with creativity researchers
- Academic researchers on specialized science podcasts
- Pioneers in computational creativity or AI creativity

## QUALITY GATES FOR THIS COURSE

**MUST HAVE:**
- At least 2-3 academic researchers (psychology, neuroscience, cognitive science)
- At least 2-3 design thinking practitioners (IDEO, d.school, etc.)
- At least 2-3 successful founders/innovators who can speak to creative process
- Mix of perspectives: science + practice + real-world application
- NO spiritual/mystical creativity content
- NO generic "be more creative" self-help without credentials

**DIVERSITY TARGETS:**
- Different podcasts for each episode (18 total)
- Different guests for each episode (avoid repeating same expert)
- Different angles: psychology, neuroscience, design, business, art, technology
- Different frameworks: design thinking, divergent/convergent, constraints, collaboration, etc.

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

This course succeeds if:
1. All episodes feature identifiable experts with clear credentials
2. Mix of academic rigor (researchers) and practical application (designers, founders)
3. Clear progression from "what is creativity" → "how to do it" → "cutting-edge research"
4. 18 different podcasts showing breadth of available content
5. No overlap in guests (diverse expert panel)
6. Each episode has specific frameworks, techniques, or insights (not generic inspiration)
7. Listeners leave with both understanding (science) and tools (frameworks)
