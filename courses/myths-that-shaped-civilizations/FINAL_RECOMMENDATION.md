## LESSONS LEARNED FOR FUTURE COURSES

1. **Mythology = Storytelling Bias**
   - Most mythology podcasts are narrative/storytelling
   - Academic expert interviews are rare
   - This pattern likely holds for other mythology topics

2. **Search Strategy Insights**
   - Academic names work better than topic searches
   - "Professor + topic" yields better results than just topic
   - BBC and major history podcasts (History Hit, Tides of History) are reliable sources

3. **Geographic Challenges**
   - Greek and Roman content abundant
   - Norse/Viking severely limited
   - Mesopotamian nearly impossible
   - Egyptian moderate availability

4. **Format Recognition**
   - Must carefully read descriptions to identify interview vs. monologue
   - BBC presentations are high quality but don't fit strict interview criteria
   - Educational podcasts may be solo host even with good content

5. **Realistic Expectations**
   - For niche academic topics, 10-15 quality episodes is realistic
   - 18+ episodes requires significant compromises OR popular topics
   - Quality should trump quantity
