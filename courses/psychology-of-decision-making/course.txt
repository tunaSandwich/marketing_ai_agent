## BEGINNER LEVEL (6 Episodes)

### Episode 1
**Episode ID:** 1488761
**Title:** "Daniel Kahneman: Thinking Fast and Slow"
**Podcast ID:** 37010
**Podcast Name:** Long Now Foundation

**Guest:** Daniel Kahneman
**Credentials:** Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences (2002), Professor of Psychology (Princeton University), Author of "Thinking, Fast and Slow," Pioneer of behavioral economics

**Expertise Signals:**
- Nobel laureate for integrating psychological research into economic science
- Princeton University professor
- Decades of research on judgment and decision-making
- Most cited psychologist alive

**Why This Episode (Level):**
This is THE foundational episode for understanding decision-making psychology. Kahneman introduces System 1 (fast, intuitive, automatic) vs. System 2 (slow, deliberate, effortful) thinking—the core framework that underlies all modern understanding of cognitive biases. The Long Now Foundation format makes these revolutionary insights accessible to general audiences while maintaining intellectual rigor. Perfect starting point because every other episode in this course builds on Kahneman's dual-process framework.

**What Listeners Will Learn:**
- The two systems of thinking and when each is activated
- Why intuitive judgments are often wrong but feel right
- Common cognitive shortcuts (heuristics) and where they fail
- Foundation for understanding all subsequent biases and decision-making research

**Why This Guest Is Credible:**
Kahneman revolutionized our understanding of human judgment, winning the Nobel Prize for showing that people are systematically irrational. His research forms the foundation of behavioral economics. He is universally recognized as one of the most influential psychologists of all time.

### Episode 2
**Episode ID:** 11639966
**Title:** "Dr. Barry Schwartz, The Paradox of Choice"
**Podcast ID:** 143539
**Podcast Name:** Unknown Podcast

**Guest:** Barry Schwartz
**Credentials:** Professor of Psychology (Swarthmore College), Author of "The Paradox of Choice: Why More Is Less," TED speaker with millions of views, Named by Business Week and Forbes

**Expertise Signals:**
- Professor at prestigious liberal arts college
- Published 100+ articles in professional journals
- Book named top business book by Business Week and Forbes Magazine
- Popular TED talk on the paradox of choice

**Why This Episode (Level):**
Choice overload is something everyone experiences daily—from Netflix paralysis to supermarket overwhelm. Schwartz makes his research immediately relatable through everyday examples. Introduces the critical distinction between "maximizers" (who seek the absolute best) and "satisficers" (who seek "good enough"), showing how maximizers are consistently less happy despite better outcomes. Perfect for beginners because it's instantly applicable and doesn't require understanding complex theories—just recognition of a universal experience.

**What Listeners Will Learn:**
- Why more choice often leads to paralysis and regret
- The difference between maximizing and satisficing strategies
- How to make decisions more efficiently and feel better about them
- The psychological cost of opportunity cost

**Why This Guest Is Credible:**
Schwartz conducted rigorous research on decision-making and happiness over decades at Swarthmore. His work on choice overload has been replicated and extended by researchers worldwide. His ability to communicate complex findings to general audiences made "The Paradox of Choice" an influential bestseller.
