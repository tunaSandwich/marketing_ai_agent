# WORLD WAR II: FROM ORIGINS TO AFTERMATH - FINAL COURSE STRUCTURE
## 18 Episodes from 18 Different Podcasts - All with Verified Expert Credentials

---

## COURSE DESCRIPTION

This comprehensive World War II course takes learners on an authoritative journey from the war's origins through its aftermath, guided exclusively by PhD historians, university professors, and published scholars. Each episode features expert interviews examining not just what happened, but why—and how WWII shaped the modern world.

The course progresses through three levels:
- **Beginner**: Introduces major figures, turning points, and foundational events
- **Intermediate**: Explores military operations, strategy, and the home front
- **Advanced**: Analyzes consequences, moral questions, and lasting global impact

Every historian featured has verifiable academic credentials, published scholarship, or direct participation in historic events. This ensures learners receive authoritative, rigorously researched perspectives on history's most consequential conflict.

### EPISODE 13: The Nuremberg Trials - Birth of International Justice
**Episode ID:** 231613
**Podcast:** BBC Witness
**Podcast ID:** 6226

**Expert:** Benjamin Ferencz
**Credentials:** Chief Prosecutor for Einsatzgruppen Trial at Nuremberg, Harvard Law School graduate
**Role:** Only surviving American prosecutor from Nuremberg Trials

**Why Selected:**
Ferencz prosecuted the Einsatzgruppen (mobile killing units) and provides firsthand account of how Nuremberg established that leaders can be held accountable for war crimes and crimes against humanity—foundations of modern international law.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand Nuremberg's legal innovations
- Grasp how evidence of Nazi crimes was documented
- Recognize establishment of "crimes against humanity"
- Connect to International Criminal Court foundations

**Guest Expertise:** Direct participant, Chief Prosecutor, Harvard Law, human rights pioneer
