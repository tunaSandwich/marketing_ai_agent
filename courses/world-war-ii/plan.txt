# World War II: From Origins to Aftermath - Course Plan

## Course Vision
A sweeping, narrative-driven course that walks learners through the events, people, and consequences of World War II. Starting with the political tensions and propaganda that sparked the conflict, progressing through decisive battles and intelligence operations, and finishing with social and moral legacies. Each episode helps learners understand not just what happened, but why—and how it shaped the modern world.

## Quality Standards: STRICT ACADEMIC CRITERIA
**ONLY episodes featuring:**
- PhD historians or professors specializing in WWII
- Published authors with recognized WWII scholarship
- Museum directors/curators with academic credentials
- Military historians with formal training
- Researchers affiliated with major institutions

**MUST HAVE in description:**
- Clear identification of guest expert
- Specific credentials (PhD, Professor at X University, Author of Y book, etc.)
- Interview format (not host monologue)

**AUTOMATIC REJECTION:**
- Host monologues without expert guests
- "Amateur historians" (regardless of popularity)
- Survivor testimonies alone (powerful but not expert analysis)
- Vague credentials or no credentials mentioned
- General discussion podcasts

## Course Structure: Three Levels

### BEGINNER LEVEL (6 episodes)
**Focus:** Origins, major turning points, and key figures
**Topics to cover:**
1. Rise of Hitler and Nazi Germany
2. Pearl Harbor and America's entry into the war
3. D-Day and the Normandy invasion
4. Churchill's leadership during Britain's darkest hour
5. The atomic bombs and Japan's surrender
6. The Holocaust: understanding the unthinkable

**Guest profile:** Historians who can make complex geopolitics accessible, explain "why" things happened, provide context for general audiences.

### INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (6 episodes)
**Focus:** Strategic decisions, military operations, home front
**Topics to cover:**
1. Battle of Stalingrad: turning point in the East
2. The intelligence war: codebreakers and espionage
3. The Pacific Theater: island-hopping and naval warfare
4. Resistance movements and underground fighters
5. War production and the home front transformation
6. FDR's wartime diplomacy and the Big Three

**Guest profile:** Military historians, strategic analysts, scholars who understand the mechanics of how the war was fought and won.

### ADVANCED LEVEL (6 episodes)
**Focus:** Consequences, moral questions, lasting impact
**Topics to cover:**
1. The Nuremberg Trials and birth of international law
2. The Marshall Plan and rebuilding Europe
3. Cold War origins: Yalta and Potsdam conferences
4. Decolonization accelerated by WWII
5. Memory and memorialization: how we remember the war
6. WWII's impact on civil rights and social change

**Guest profile:** Scholars examining long-term consequences, moral philosophers, international relations experts, historians of memory and commemoration.

## Expertise Recognition Checklist

For each episode, verify:
✅ **Academic Credentials in Description:**
- "Professor of History at..."
- "PhD in..."
- "Author of [book published by major press]"
- "Director of [museum/research center]"
- "Fellow of [academic society]"

✅ **Interview Format Confirmed:**
- "[Host] interviews [Expert Name]"
- "Joins us to discuss"
- "[Expert] explains"
- Guest name appears in title or description

✅ **Relevant Expertise:**
- Has published on this specific topic
- Academic position related to WWII/20th century history
- Recognized authority in the field

❌ **Reject If:**
- No guest name mentioned
- No credentials listed
- "I discuss..." (host monologue)
- Vague descriptions
- Amateur/self-taught historians

## Episode Diversity Requirements
- 18 different podcasts (maximum diversity)
- No podcast used more than once
- Geographic diversity of experts when possible
- Mix of American, British, European scholars
- Variety of institutional affiliations
