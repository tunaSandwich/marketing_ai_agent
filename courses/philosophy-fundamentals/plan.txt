# Course Plan: "Philosophy Fundamentals"

## Course Purpose
A journey through humanity's deepest questions—what we can know, what's right, and what it means to live well. You'll meet the great thinkers from Plato to Simone de Beauvoir and unpack key branches like metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, political philosophy, aesthetics, and philosophy of mind. The course concludes with modern ideas like posthumanism, simulation, and consciousness in AI, making philosophy feel alive and relevant today.

## Course Strategy

### Foundational Concepts (Beginner Level)
- What is philosophy? The fundamental questions
- Ancient Greek philosophy: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle
- Epistemology basics: What can we know? How do we know it?
- Metaphysics basics: What is reality? What exists?
- Ethics basics: What is right and wrong? How should we live?
- Making philosophy accessible through engaging storytelling

### Core Systems/Branches (Intermediate Level)
- Major philosophical movements: Existentialism, Phenomenology, Pragmatism, Stoicism
- Political philosophy: Justice, rights, democracy, the social contract
- Philosophy of mind: Consciousness, free will, personal identity, the mind-body problem
- Aesthetics: Beauty, art, meaning, creativity
- Modern philosophers: Descartes, Kant, Hume, Nietzsche, Mill, Rawls
- Ethical theories: Utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics, care ethics

### Contemporary Frontiers/Debates (Advanced Level)
- Philosophy of AI: Can machines be conscious? Digital minds and moral status
- Simulation hypothesis: Are we living in a simulation?
- Posthumanism and transhumanism: Enhancement, uploading, the future of humanity
- Meta-ethics: Moral realism vs anti-realism, the nature of moral facts
- Philosophy of technology: How technology shapes human existence
- Existential risk and longtermism: Future generations and cosmic ethics
- Philosophy of language: Meaning, reference, and communication

## Quality Assurance for Philosophy Course

### MUST HAVE:
✅ Clear philosophical credentials (Professor of Philosophy, PhD, published philosopher)
✅ Interview format (philosopher being interviewed, not solo host monologue)
✅ Academic affiliation or published works mentioned
✅ Substantive philosophical discussion (not just pop psychology)
✅ Clear expertise in the philosophical topic being discussed

### RED FLAGS:
❌ No credentials mentioned in description
❌ Self-help or motivational content disguised as philosophy
❌ Amateur podcasts with hosts discussing philosophy casually
❌ Spiritual/mystical content without philosophical rigor
❌ "Life coach" or "guru" types without academic background
❌ Solo host explaining philosophy from Wikipedia or textbooks
❌ No identifiable expert guest

### Format Indicators:
- ✅ GOOD: "[Philosopher Name], Professor of Philosophy at [University]"
- ✅ GOOD: "Author of [philosophy book]"
- ✅ GOOD: "[Host] interviews [Philosopher]"
- ❌ BAD: "We discuss philosophy" (no guest mentioned)
- ❌ BAD: "I break down [philosophical concept]" (solo host)
- ❌ BAD: "Two friends explore philosophy" (no expert)

## Expected Episode Progression

### Beginner (6 episodes):
1. What is philosophy? Big questions and why they matter
2. Ancient Greek philosophy: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle
3. Ethics: How should we live? (accessible intro to moral philosophy)
4. Epistemology: What can we know? (accessible intro to knowledge)
5. Free will vs determinism: Do we have control?
6. Meaning and purpose: Existential questions made accessible

**Format:** Well-known hosts (Lex Fridman, Sean Carroll, Sam Harris) interviewing leading philosophers
**Guests:** David Chalmers, Peter Singer, Martha Nussbaum, Daniel Dennett, etc.
**Tone:** "Make me curious about philosophy and show me why it matters"

### Intermediate (6 episodes):
1. Political philosophy: Justice, rights, and the social contract
2. Philosophy of mind: Consciousness and the hard problem
3. Existentialism and phenomenology: Sartre, Camus, de Beauvoir
4. Aesthetics: Beauty, art, and meaning
5. Modern moral philosophy: Kant, Mill, contemporary debates
6. Personal identity and the self: What makes you "you"?

**Format:** Philosophy podcasts or academic discussions with philosophers
**Guests:** Professors discussing their specialties, published philosophers
**Tone:** "Teach me the major schools of thought and key debates"

### Advanced (6 episodes):
1. Simulation hypothesis: Are we living in a simulation? (Nick Bostrom)
2. AI consciousness and digital minds: Can machines be conscious?
3. Posthumanism and transhumanism: The future of humanity
4. Meta-ethics: Are moral facts real? Realism vs anti-realism
5. Existential risk and longtermism: Ethics of the far future
6. Philosophy of language or advanced consciousness debates

**Format:** Technical philosophy discussions with cutting-edge researchers
**Guests:** Leaders in contemporary philosophy working on frontier questions
**Tone:** "Show me the current debates and unsolved problems in philosophy"
