# Course Plan: How to Parent: The Early Years

## Course Focus
A grounded, compassionate course for new and soon-to-be parents navigating the first five years of a child's life. Focus on evidence-based parenting from pediatricians, child psychologists, developmental experts, and experienced practitioners.

## Level Breakdown Strategy

### Beginner Level: Foundations (0-2 years)
Focus: The basics every new parent needs to know
- Foundational concepts: attachment, security, normal development
- Sleep basics and why it's challenging
- Feeding approaches (breast, bottle, starting solids)
- Understanding infant behavior and communication
- What's normal vs when to worry
- Parental self-care and maintaining relationships

### Intermediate Level: Developmental Milestones (2-4 years)
Focus: Understanding toddler development and behavior management
- Emotional regulation in toddlers
- Language development and communication
- Managing tantrums and difficult behaviors
- Setting boundaries and discipline approaches
- Social development and play
- Potty training and developmental milestones

### Advanced Level: Long-term Foundations (3-5 years)
Focus: Building resilience, independence, and preparing for school years
- Executive function development
- Fostering curiosity and learning
- Building resilience and handling adversity
- Preparing for school readiness
- Different parenting philosophies examined (attachment, RIE, Montessori, etc.)
- Research on long-term outcomes and what actually matters

## Expertise Indicators to Look For

### Medical/Clinical Credentials:
- MD, Pediatrician
- Child Psychologist, Clinical Psychologist
- PhD in Developmental Psychology
- PhD in Child Development
- Licensed therapist specializing in families

### Academic/Research:
- Professor of Pediatrics
- Professor of Psychology
- Researcher at [Children's Hospital/University]
- Director of [Child Development Center]
- Published research on child development

### Practitioner Credentials:
- Certified Parenting Coach (look for specific training)
- RIE Associate
- Montessori expert/trainer
- Family therapist
- Author of parenting books (evidence-based)

### Red Flags to Avoid:
- Mommy bloggers without credentials
- "Guru" or spiritual approaches
- One-size-fits-all rigid rules
- Outdated approaches (cry it out without nuance)
- Personal anecdotes without expertise
- Anti-science stances

## Quality Criteria for This Course

### Must Have:
- Clear medical, psychological, or research credentials
- Evidence-based approaches (not just opinion)
- Interview format (expert being questioned)
- Practical, actionable advice grounded in research
- Compassionate, non-judgmental tone
- Acknowledgment of different parenting situations

### Avoid:
- Mommy/daddy bloggers without expertise
- One rigid philosophy without nuance
- Judgment or parent-shaming
- Pseudoscience or "natural parenting" extremism
- Celebrity parents sharing opinions (unless also credentialed)
- Outdated 1950s approaches

### Ideal Episode Formats:
- Pediatrician interviewed about specific topic
- Developmental psychologist explaining research
- Author of evidence-based parenting book
- University researcher on child development
- Experienced clinician with years of family therapy
- Practitioner with both credentials AND experience
