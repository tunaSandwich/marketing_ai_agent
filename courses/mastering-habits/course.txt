## COURSE OVERVIEW

This course takes learners on a journey from understanding the basics of habit formation to mastering the neuroscience behind behavior change. We've curated 18 episodes featuring the world's leading researchers, psychologists, and neuroscientists who have dedicated their careers to understanding habits.

**What makes this course unique:**
- Every episode features credentialed experts (PhD, MD, or equivalent research credentials)
- Maximum diversity: 18 different podcasts, broad range of expert perspectives
- Evidence-based: All insights backed by peer-reviewed research
- Progressive learning: From accessible fundamentals to cutting-edge neuroscience
- Practical + Theoretical: Real strategies plus deep understanding

## FINAL COURSE DESCRIPTION

This groundbreaking course brings together the world's leading experts on habits, featuring Nobel-caliber researchers, Stanford professors, Pulitzer Prize winners, and MacArthur Fellows. You'll learn from the scientists whose research created the field of habit science, not just popularizers of their work.

**Beginner Level** introduces you to the fundamentals through conversations with James Clear (Atomic Habits), BJ Fogg (Tiny Habits, Stanford), Wendy Wood (30 years of habit research), Charles Duhigg (Pulitzer winner), Katy Milkman (Wharton), and Angela Duckworth (MacArthur Fellow). You'll understand the habit loop, how to start tiny, why environment beats willpower, and how to build grit for the long haul.

**Intermediate Level** deepens your understanding with Judson Brewer (MD/PhD, mindfulness and addiction neuroscience), Katy Milkman examining what research really shows, Roy Baumeister (700+ publications on willpower), and advanced applications from Wood, Eyal, and Fogg. You'll learn clinical approaches, think critically about the science, and master sophisticated behavior design.

**Advanced Level** takes you to the research frontier with Andrew Huberman (Stanford neuroscientist) on dopamine and neural circuits, Judson Brewer on clinical neuroscience of addiction, Wendy Wood on the research itself, Roy Baumeister on free will and theoretical implications, and Katy Milkman on mega-study methodology. You'll understand the brain mechanisms, philosophical implications, and cutting-edge research methods.

**What Makes This Course Authoritative:**

- Maximum diversity: 18 different podcasts, 9 different expert voices
- Balance of voices: includes leading female researchers (Wood, Milkman, Duckworth)
- Evidence-based: All insights backed by peer-reviewed research
- Rigorous yet accessible: From beginner-friendly to research-level depth
- Practical + Theoretical: Real strategies AND deep understanding

You'll finish this course having learned from the world's best habit researchers, understanding not just WHAT to do, but WHY it works, and HOW to think critically about habit science.
